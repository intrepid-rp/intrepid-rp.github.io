Psion

Through rigorous mental training and discipline, Psions delve into the depths of the subconscious to unleash devastating mental assaults, manipulate the environment, and peer into the unseen. With their innate psychic abilities, Psions unlock a wide array of abilities, ranging from telekinesis and telepathy to precognition and psychokinesis. Agile both in body and mind, they navigate the battlefield with finesse, they navigate the complexities of combat and diplomacy with uncanny precision, making them invaluable allies and formidable adversaries on any quest.

Class Features

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d8 per paladin level.
  • Hit Points: 8 + CON at first level.
    Add 1d8 + CON at new levels.

Proficiencies

  • Armor: Light Armor
  • Weapons: Simple weapons
  • Tools: One of your choice
  • Saving Throws: DEX, INT
  • Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Arcana, History, Insight, Perception, and Religion

Starting Equipment

  • Any simple weapon
  • (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • 2 daggers

Spellcasting (Level 1)

Cantrips. You know three cantrips of your choice from the psion spell list. You learn additional psion cantrips of your choice at higher levels, as shown in the Psion Progression table.

Learning Spells. You know a number of spells from the psion spell list based on your INT and psion level. You learn additional spells each time you gain a level in this class, choosing only from spell tiers for which you have spell slots. When you gain levels in this class, you can choose one of the psion spells you know and replace it with another spell from the psion spell list.

Spells known = INT + Psion Level

Casting Spells. The Psion Progression table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your psion spells. To cast one of these spells, you must expend a slot of the spell's tier or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest. You do not require a spellcasting focus.

Spellcasting Ability. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your psion spells. You use your INT modifier when making a spell attack roll or setting the saving throw DC for a spell you cast.

Spell save DC = 8 + PB + INT
Spell attack modifier = PB + INT

Psychic Strike (Levels 1, 5, 9, and 14)

As an action, you can channel a wave of psychic energy toward a creature within 60 feet of you. When you do so, make a ranged spell attack against the target. On a hit, the target takes 1d10 psychic damage.

You can strike more than once at higher levels: twice at 5th level, three times at 9th, and four times at 14th. You make a separate attack roll for each strike, which can be directed toward different targets.

Telepathy (Level 2)

You gain the ability to communicate telepathically with any creature you see within 120 feet. If you do not have a language in common, you communicate via simple emotions and concepts.

Psion Subclass (Levels 3, 7, 11, and 15)

Choose a subclass that reflects the way in which your psionic abilities manifest themselves. Your choice grants you additional features as you gain levels.

Talented (Levels 4, 8, 12, 16, and 19)

Choose a talent from the Magic Talents list or increase one ability modifier by +1.

Psionic Manifestation (Levels 6, 13, 17)

Choose one of the following psionic manifestations. You will choose additional manifestations are higher levels.

  • Discernment. You can see the true form of any creature or object concealed by illusion or transmutation magic while the target is within 20 feet.
  • Force Push. When you make a Shove attack on your turn, you use your INT modifier for the contested skill check and, if successful, the target is pushed 20 feet and knocked prone.
  • Mind's Eye. You can see normally in darkness, both magical and nonmagical, to a distance of 120 feet.
  • Telepathic Senses. You can use your action to touch a willing creature and communicate telepathically and perceive through each other's senses as long as you are on the same plane of existence. As a bonus action on your turn, you can decide to change whose senses are shared with whom, or drop the connection. Unless you end the effect, it lasts until you complete a long rest.
  • Primal Bond. You can spend 1 hour forming a bond with a Tiny or Small non-magical beast who becomes your companion. You can perceive through your companion's senses as long as you are on the same plane of existence. The beast can understand your telepathic messages to it, and it can telepathically communicate simple emotions and concepts back to you. Your companion adds your INT modifier to its attack and damage rolls against targets you can see.
  • Precognition. You add your INT to initiative rolls in additional to your usual initiative modifier. When combat begins, you can choose any place in the initiative order that is equal to or lower than your roll.
  • Psychometry. You gain the ability to perceive information about objects or people by touching them as an action. You receive impressions or insights about their history or significance, their surface thoughts (what is most on its mind in that moment), and where they have been in the last 24 hours. The target is unaware of the effect.

Heroic Boon (Level 10)

You know the Levitate spell and can cast it on yourself as a bonus action without spending a spell slot. It does not count against your number of known spells. If you already know the Levitate spell, you learn another spell.

Mind Over Matter (Level 18)

Your mind's profound control over your physical form grants you the following benefits:

  • You gain resistance to non-magical bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.
  • You no longer age.
  • You are immune to disease and poison.

Epic Boon (Level 20)

You gain a flying speed equal to your walking speed.

Psion Subclasse

Oracle

Oracles possess a foresight that allows them to detect hidden dangers and uncover secrets. They protect allies with their prescient abilities, cast specialist spells effortlessly, and evade divination magic, making them incredible seers and protectors.

Prescient Reflexes (Level 3)

Your ability to see slightly into the future helps you identify danger and respond with supernatural speed. When you are not wearing armor, you gain a bonus to your AC.

AC = 10 + DEX + INT

You can't be affected by the Surprised condition and other creatures' attacks against you do not gain advantage as a result of surprise or being hidden.

Specialist Spells (Level 7)

You learn two of the following spells and can cast it as a bonus action on your turn. These spells do not count against your number of known spells. If you choose a spell you already know, you learn another spell from the Psion spell list.

  • Detect Evil and Good
  • Detect Poison and Disease
  • Detect Thoughts
  • Locate
  • See Invisibility

Aura of Foresight (Level 11)

You and your allies within 20 feet of you gain a bonus to all saving throws equal to your INT modifier.

Clairvoyant (Level 15)

You are hidden from divination magic. You can't be targeted by such magic or perceived through magical scrying sensors. You learn the Clairvoyance spell if you don't already know it, and can cast it as an action on your turn.

Telekinetic

Telekinetics wield their mind as a weapon, enhancing physical attacks with mental prowess and psionic spells. They move with increased agility and strike with precision, pushing enemies back and leaping great distances effortlessly.

Martial Training (Level 3)

You gain proficiency with Medium Armor and Martial Weapons, and your weapon attacks use your INT instead of your DEX or STR modifier.

When you gain a new talent, you can select it from either the Magic or the Martial talent lists.

Your speed increases by 5 feet.

Specialist Spells (Level 7)

You learn two of the following spells and can cast it as a bonus action on your turn. These spells do not count against your number of known spells. If you choose a spell you already know, you learn another spell from the Psion spell list.

  • Catapult
  • Earthbind
  • Jump
  • Levitate
  • Longstrider
  • Spider Climb
  • Thunderwave

Quick Attack (Level 11)

You can use your Psychic Strike feature as a melee attack. When you do so successfully, you can choose to also push the target back 5 feet if they fail a STR save against your spell DC.

When you attempt a melee attack against a creature, you don't provoke opportunity attacks from that creature for the rest of the turn.

Your speed increases by 5 feet.

Psionic Leap (Level 15)

You are permanently under the effect of the Jump spell. If you know the Jump spell, you can choose to replace it with another spell.

Telepath

Telepaths master the art of mind control and mental communication. They enchant enemies without detection, create telepathic links with allies, and respond to attacks with psychic retaliation, making them both formidable and elusive.

Enchantment Adept (Level 3)

Add INT to WIS saves to resist spells or effects that charm, dominate, control your will, or detect your thoughts. If you succeed on the save, the aggressor is unaware that their spell failed until you make it obvious by disobeying their commands. When you cast Enchantment spell that would normally alert the target to the fact they had been targeted, they are instead unaware of your attempt to enchant them.

Specialist Spells (Level 7)

You learn two of the following spells and can cast it as a bonus action on your turn. These spells do not count against your number of known spells. If you choose a spell you already know, you learn another spell from the Psion spell list.

  • Bane
  • Calm Emotions
  • Fear
  • Charm
  • Detect Thoughts
  • Enthrall
  • Suggestion

Telepathic Aura (Level 11)

A telepathic aura emanates from you in a 60 ft radius, affecting up to 8 willing creatures you choose who gain the ability to communicate with each other telepathically. They must know at least one language but do not need to share a common language in order to communicate. Affected creatures also know each other's location while within range. You can include or exclude creatures from the aura's effect as a bonus action on your turn.

Psychic Riposte (Level 15)

You learn the Hellish Rebuke spell which you can cast as a reaction when you take damage from a creature within 60 feet that you can see. You can also use this reaction any time an enemy forces you to make a saving throw and you succeed, even when the effect does not cause damage.
