Witch

A witch is a master of wyrd magic, deeply connected to the spiritual world, with the unique ability to craft enchanted objects and jinx their enemies. They can specialize in a wide array of witchcraft, from brewing powerful potions to creating charms, dreamcatchers, or masks. Witches are often members of secretive covens which help pass down knowledge and provide mutual protection. Their association with the mystical world allows them to commune with infernal, demonic, celestial, or fey spirits. With the ability to harm, heal, deceive, and frighten, witches are versatile adventurers.

Class Features

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d8 per witch level.
  • Hit Points: 8 + CON at first level.
    Add 1d8 + CON at new levels.

Proficiencies

  • Armor: Light armor
  • Weapons: Simple weapons
  • Tools: Herbalism Kit
  • Saving Throws: WIS, CHA
  • Skills: Choose two from Arcana, Deception, Medicine, Nature, Perception, and Persuasion

Starting Equipment

  • (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • a wyrd focus and a ceremonial dagger

Spellcasting (Level 1)

You have the ability to cast Wyrd spells.

Cantrips. At 1st level, choose four cantrips from the Wyrd spell list to learn. You choose more Wyrd cantrips to learn at higher levels, as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Witch Progression table.

Casting Spells. You know a small number of spells and can cast any of them by using a Wyrd spell slot of the spell's tier or higher. You don't need to prepare spells ahead of time. The Witch Progression table shows how many spells you know and how many spell slots you have at a given level. You regain all used spell slots when you finish a long rest.

Spells Known. At 1st level, choose two 1st-tier spells from the Wyrd spell list to learn. The Spells Known column of the Witch Progression table shows when you learn additional Wyrd spells. Each spell you learn must be from a tier for which you have spell slots. When you gain a witch level, you can choose one Wyrd spell you know and replace it with another spell of your choice from the Wyrd spell list.

Spellcasting Ability. Charisma (CHA) is your spellcasting ability. Your CHA modifier determines the save DC or the attack modifier for certain spells you cast:

  • Spell save DC = 8 + PB + CHA
  • Spell attack modifier = PB + CHA

Ritual Casting. As a witch, you can also cast a long-form type of spell called a ritual. You do not need to prepare ritual spells, and they do not use up spell slots. At 1st level, choose one ritual from the Wyrd ritual list. You choose more Wyrd rituals to learn at higher levels, as shown in the Rituals Known column of the Witch Progression table. You can only learn a ritual spell from a tier you have spell slots for. Rituals you learn do not count against your number of spells known.

Witchcrafting (Level 1)

You are able to craft one type of witch's tool imbued with wyrd magic. Choose one type of witchcrafted tool from the list below. These magical items can be created or replaced through a 1-hour ritual and require attunement. At higher levels, you can learn to craft a new type of tool or improve one you've already crafted.

  • Cauldron. A small three-legged iron pot consecrated for use in rituals, brewing potions, and burning incense or herbs. You spend half the time to cast ritual spells. Once per short rest when targeted by an attacker you see, you can cast the Fog Cloud or Darkness spell as a reaction. You can craft one potion per long rest which lasts 24 hours. Choose from Antitoxin, Climbing, Fire Breath, Healing, Poison, or Strength.

  • Charm. Protective amulets and empowering talismans come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and materials. Some are very simple while others can be engraved with intricate designs or inscribed with sigils or runes. You craft one of the following charms.
    • Amulet of Protection. You gain a +1 bonus to AC and saving throws while wearing this amulet and you are hidden from divination magic.
    • Amulet of Shielding. You know the Shield spell and can use it to protect creatures within 10 feet of you who are targeted by an attack. You can cast the spell once per day using the amulet's power instead of a spell slot.
    • Talisman of Resilience. Your CON is +4 while wearing this amulet. You have advantage on saving throws made against poison or harmful gases.
    • Talisman of Agility. Your jump distance is tripled and difficult terrain doesn't cost you extra movement. You have advantage on stealth checks that rely on moving silently.

  • Doll. A small humanoid-shaped figurine made of natural materials, used to perform sympathetic magic where spells cast on the doll effect the person it represents. As a bonus action on your turn, select a target within 30 feet. Your doll is sympathetically linked to this target and you can cast spells with a range of touch against this target. You know the Cure Wounds spell.

  • Dreamcatcher. A handmade set of willow hoops on which are woven nets or webs, decorated with sacred items like feathers or beads. These hoops ward your unconscious thoughts.
    • Mind Ward. You use the higher of your CHA and WIS modifiers when calculating a WIS save. You gain resistance to psychic damage.
    • Peaceful Repose. When you regain HP at the end of a short rest, you gain the benefit of an extra hit die.
  • Mask. Unique masks in a wide range of styles ranging from elegant to ghastly.
    • Beast. You have advantage on Perception checks. You grow natural weapons that deal damage equal to your jinx die. You are proficient with these natural weapons which are magical and have a +1 attack and damage bonus.
    • Monster. You can use your action to frighten one target within 60 feet. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become frightened of you for up to 1 minute. It can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success and becoming immune to the effect for one day.
    • Trickster. You can disguise both your appearance and your voice for one hour per day. You can use this feature PB times per long rest.

  • Orb. A small polished sphere of quartz or obsidian serving as a focal point for divination magic. You can replace the material component of divination spells with your orb. The orb awakens when danger is imminent. Add your CHA to your Initiative roll modifier. You know the Locate spell and can cast it once per day without using a spell slot.

  • Tattoo. You gain powers through one of the following tattoos.
    • Angel. Once per long rest when you are reduced to 0 HP, you are reduced to 1 HP instead.
    • Armor. Your unarmored AC equals 13 + DEX.
    • Elemental. Choose one damage type from fire, cold, lightning, thunder, or acid. You gain resistance to this damage type. Your attacks do additional damage of this type equal to your Jinx die.
    • Hand. You know the Mage Hand cantrip.
    • Mask. You know the Disguise Self spell and can cast it a number of times equal to your PB per long rest.
    • Necromancer. You are resistant to necrotic damage. You can make a melee spell attack to deal necrotic damage equal to your jinx die. You regain half that amount of HP, rounded down.
    • Spellweaver. You gain a +1 bonus to your spell attacks and spell save DC.
    • Weapon. You bond with one weapon which you can summon or dismiss as a bonus action. It is a +1 magical weapon and you can use your CHA modifier when attacking with it.
  • Wand. A slender intricately crafted wooden rod used to focus magical energy. A wand you craft has a +1 bonus to your spell attacks and spell save DC. You empower the wand with one metamagic option from the Sorcerer class. It has a number of charges equal to your PB which you use instead of sorcery points.

Jinx (Levels 2, 6, 10, 14)

When a creature you see within 60 feet makes an ability check, attack, or saving throw, you can use your reaction to jinx the creature. The creature subtracts your jinx die from its roll. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your PB per long rest. Your jinx die changes when you reach certain levels, as shown in the Witch Progression table.

Coven (Levels 3, 7, 11, and 15)

Choose a coven that reflects the type of like-minded witches with whom you find community, knowledge, or protection. Your choice grants you features as you gain levels.

Talented (Levels 4, 8, 12, 16, and 19)

Choose a talent from the Magic Talents list or increase one ability modifier by +1.

Greater Witchcrafting (Levels 5, 9, 13, and 17)

You can craft a new type of tool or choose from the following tool improvements:

  • Cauldron. You have advantage on checks to maintain concentration on the Fog Cloud or Darkness spells and you can see normally through these spells' effects when you cast them. You can craft two potions instead of one per long rest.
  • Charm. You can craft another charm of a different type.
  • Doll. The distance of your sympathetic bond increases to 60 feet. You know the Command and Hold Person spells.
  • Dreamcatcher. Your mind ward extends to allies within 30 feet. Your allies benefit from an extra hit die at the end of a short rest.
  • Mask. You can upgrade a mask or create a new one.
    • Beast. You have expertise on Perception checks. Your natural weapons gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls.
    • Monster. You know the Fear spell.
    • Trickster. Your disguise lasts until dispelled.
  • Orb. You upgrade your orb. You know the Clairvoyance spell and can cast it once per day without using a spell slot.
  • Tattoo. Choose another type of tattoo.
  • Wand. You can craft an additional wand or add another type of meta magic to your existing wand.

Heroic Boon (Level 10)

Your commitment to your coven grants you a powerful new ability. Choose one of the following heroic boons:

  • Witch's Lair. Once per day, you can conjure a lair that takes up no more than a 15 foot cube. The interior is dry and the temperature is comfortable regardless of conditions outside. It includes basic furniture, plants, and decorations of your choice as well as a witchcrafting workbench, a herbalism kit, and other tools with which you are proficient. The conjured lair lasts for 24 hours or until you choose dismiss it. When the spell ends, all creatures or objects left inside are expelled and your lair disappears into a pocket dimension.
  • Witch's Conveyance. You summon a spirit that inhabits an object to be used as a means of flying. Brooms and rugs are commonly used but witches have also been known to use umbrellas, planks of wood, spears, tombstones, and other objects. It has a flying speed of 50 feet. The broom can follow your simple mental instructions as long as it remains within 1 mile of you.

Coven Archwitch (Level 18)

You are invested with the knowledge of witch queens that have gone before you. You can choose from any spell list when you learn new spells.

You form a unique bond with a powerful infernal, demonic, celestial, or fey spiritual being. Once per day as an action, you can call on them to reveal information to you as described in the Scrying or Commune spells. Alternatively, you can call them to your aid as in the Planar Ally spell. These are natural abilities based on your connection to your spiritual ally and do not require a spell slot.

Epic Boon (Level 20)

Your mastery of witchcraft has reached its peak. You gain the following epic boon:

  • Haunting Aura. Once per day, you can use your action to exude a protective aura in a 30 ft radius for up to one hour. You call on the aid of infernal, demonic, celestial, or fey spirits and your countenance becomes especially horrific or beatific. When an enemy first enters your aura, they must succeed on a WIS save or take 6d8 damage and become frightened of you for one minute. On a successful save, the enemy takes half damage and is not frightened. They can repeat the save at the end of each turn. To make combat more efficient, roll your aura damage once and apply the same damage each time a creature enters your aura.

Witch Subclasses

Coven of the Dreamwalkers

Dreamwalker witches draw power from the subconscious. They have learned to quiet the noise of their senses and to trust their intuition to perceive the truths that lie beneath the surface. They embrace the bizarre and beautiful ways of the mind and the collective unconscious that links all beings. These witches rarely meet each other physically, meeting instead in a dream realm they call The Reverie. Each coven member's personal dreamscape within The Reverie is linked to their coven. For example, a door within one witch's castle dreamscape may open onto another witch's walled garden.

Sleep Mastery (Level 3)

You know the Sleep spell. When you use an action to cast it, you call forth spirits of the dream realm to heighten the spell's effect. You roll an additional 2d8 to determine how much HP the spell can affect.

You cannot be unwillingly subjected to spells or powers that affect your sleep or dreams such as the Sleep or Dream spells, the sleep effects of the Eyebite or Symbol spells, or the Nightmare Haunting power of a Night Hag.

Dreamwalker (Level 7)

You teleport into a space adjacent to a creature within 120 feet with an intelligence score of 6 or higher, led to their thoughts through the dream realm. An unwilling target whose thoughts you have not already detected (through the Detect Thoughts or Detect Good or Evil spells, for example) can make a WIS saving throw to resist the effect. On a successful save, you travel only half the distance to the target. You can do this a number of times up to your PB per long rest.

Dreamweaver (Level 11)

When you use the Dream spell to shape a creature's dreams, you gain the following benefits when pulling the target into your personal dreamscape:

  • For each spell tier above 5th, you can target one additional creature and your monstrous messenger deals +1d6 psychic damage.
  • The target of your spell has disadvantage on the WIS save.
  • You can target creatures who don't sleep as long as they are in a trance state, however they do not suffer disadvantage on the WIS save.

Surreal Aura (Level 15)

Once per day, you can channel a dreamlike aura, causing those around you to become awestruck or frightened. As an action, you can exude a dreamlike or nightmare aura (your choice) to a distance of 60 feet. For 1 minute or until you lose your concentration (as if you were casting a concentration spell), each hostile creature that starts its turn in this aura must succeed on a WIS saving throw or become charmed or frightened until the aura ends. A creature that succeeds on its saving throw is immune to your aura for 24 hours.

Coven of the Familiar Masters

Some witches focus on the ancient mystic art of forming unique, unbreakable bonds with powerful spirits summoned into animal form. Unlike common familiars, these summoned companions are more than mere servants. They are an extension of a witch's power and will, growing in strength and intelligence alongside their master, becoming formidable allies in battle and wise advisors in times of need.

Familiar Master (Level 3)

You know the Find Familiar ritual and can cast it using an action to cast it as a spell rather than performing the ritual. This does not require a spell slot and doesn't count against your number of known spells. The spirit you summon is more powerful than a typical familiar and gains the following benefits:

  • Its size is Tiny or Small and takes the form of a Beast or Plant up to CR 1; or an Imp, Quasit, or Pseudodragon.
  • It uses your PB for saves and ability checks.
  • It adds your PB to its usual armor class.
  • Its HP is equal to your CHA modifier + three times your witch level. For example, a second level witch with +3 CHA would summon a familiar with 9 HP (3+(3*2)).
  • Its attacks are enhanced by your bond. It uses your spell attack bonus or its normal attack bonus, whichever is higher.
  • Its attacks deal force damage equal to 1d4 + its STR or DEX + your PB. Additional damage or effects from its attacks still apply (extra poison damage, grappling, etc).
  • Its INT modifier is 8 unless it's already higher.
  • It can understand and speak Common and either Celestial, Sylvan, Abyssal, or Infernal depending on its origins.
  • You can access your familiar's senses as a bonus action.

Teleportation Bond (Level 7)

You can teleport your familiar to your side as a bonus action. You can use your action to disappear in a puff of smoke and teleport to your familiar as long as it is within 60 feet of you. You can do this PB times per day.

Cooperative Spellcasting (Level 11)

You can cast ranged spells through your familiar as long as it is within 60 feet of you. The spell originates from your familiar's position and the spell range is limited to 30 feet.

Familiar Aura (Level 15)

As an action on your turn, you can have your familiar emit one of two auras for 1 minute. You can take this action PB times per day.

  • Bless. Your familiar emits aura of daylight in a 20-foot radius. Allies within the aura gain a 1d4 bonus to attacks, saves, and ability checks.
  • Bane. Your familiar emits an aura of chaotic energy in a 20-foot radius. Enemies who enter the aura for the first time must succeed on a WIS save based on your spell save DC. If they fail the save, they subtract 1d4 from their attacks, saves, and ability checks a long as they are within the aura.
