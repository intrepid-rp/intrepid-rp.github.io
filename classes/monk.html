Monk

These nimble combatants cultivate their skills through years of rigorous training, whether within secluded monasteries or through solitary dedication. Masters of unarmed combat, monks eschew heavy armor and weaponry, opting instead for the artistry of unarmed strikes and martial finesse. With lightning reflexes and unparalleled martial arts techniques, they navigate the battlefield with grace and precision, embodying the epitome of martial excellence. Whether as stalwart allies or formidable adversaries, monks command respect and admiration, their skill in combat a testament to their dedication and training.

Class Features

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d10 per druid level.
  • Hit Points: 10 + CON at first level.
    Add 1d10 + CON at new levels.

Proficiencies

  • Armor: None
  • Weapons: Simple weapons, Finesse weapons
  • Tools: One of your choice
  • Saving Throws: DEX, WIS
  • Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, Insight, Perception, Religion, and Stealth

Starting Equipment

  • (a) any simple weapon or (b) a martial finesse weapon
  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • (a) 10 darts or (b) 2 daggers

Unarmored Defense (Level 1)

While you are wearing no armor and not wielding a shield, your AC equals 10 + DEX + WIS.

Martial Arts (Level 1)

You have mastered combat styles that use unarmed strikes and monk weapons, which are shortswords and any simple melee weapons. When you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons, and you aren't using heavy armor or shields:

  • You can replace the normal damage from you unarmed strikes or weapon attacks with your martials arts die, which changes as you gain monk levels.
  • You can use DEX instead of STR for attack and damage rolls of you unarmed strikes and monk weapons.
  • When you use the Attack action with an unarmed strike or a monk weapon on your turn, you can make one unarmed strike as a bonus action.
  • When you are hit by a ranged weapon attack, you can use your reaction to deflect or catch the missile. The damage you take from the attack is reduced by 1d10 + DEX + monk level. If you reduce the damage to 0, you catch the missile (if it's small enough to hold in a free hand) and can then make a ranged attack with it as part of the same reaction. You are proficient with this attack which uses your martial arts die for damage and has a normal range of 20 feet and a long range of 60 feet.

Ki Mastery (Level 2)

Your training allows you to harness the mystic energy of your life force. Characters may refer to this energy by different names (Ki, Prana, Pneuma, Mana, etc) or they may access this power intuitively without having a word for it. The game mechanics will refer to "Ki" but your character will call it what they want. Your access to this energy is represented by a number of ki points.

Ki Point Total = Your monk level + WIS

You can spend these points to fuel various ki techniques which you learn as you gain monk levels. You recover expended ki points by meditating for 30 minutes as part of a short or long rest.

If a ki feature requires your target to make a saving throw, the save DC is calculated as follows:

Ki Save DC = 8 + PB + WIS

You know three of the following Ki Techniques:

  • Flurry of Blows. Immediately after you take the Attack action on your turn, you can spend 1 ki point to make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action.
  • Flurry of Throws. Immediately after making a thrown weapon attack on your turn, you can spend 1 ki point to make two thrown weapon attacks as a bonus action.
  • Patient Defense. When you are targeted by an attack you can see, you may spend 1 ki point to take the Dodge action using a reaction.
  • Step of the Wind. You can spend 1 ki point to both Disengage and Dash as a bonus action on your turn, and your jump distance is doubled.
  • Whirling Defense. When you take the Dodge action on your turn, you can spend 1 ki point and deploy a dynamic defense through continuous weapon movement, gaining a +2 bonus to your AC until the start of your next turn.
  • Springing Strike. You can spend 1 ki point to increase the reach of your melee attacks by 5 feet until the start of your next turn.
  • Parry. When you are hit with a melee attack, you can spend 1 ki point to use your reaction and reduce the damage by an amount equal to your DEX + martial arts die.
  • Riposte. When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can spend 1 ki point to use your reaction and make a melee weapon attack against the creature. Add one extra Martial Arts die to the damage roll.
  • Crushing Blow. Once per turn, you can spend 1 ki point to cause extra damage equal to your Martial Arts die.

Unarmored Movement (Level 2)

Your speed increases by 10 feet while you are not wearing armor or wielding a shield. This bonus increases when you reach certain monk levels, as shown in the Monk table.

Monk Subclass (Levels 3, 7, 11, and 15)

You commit yourself to a distinct type of monk training, or subclass, offering distinct abilities and powers reflective of your character's unique training or traditions. Whether honed in the solitude of remote monasteries or under the guidance of a revered master, each subclass embodies a particular philosophy or approach to martial arts and mysticism. These subclasses grant practitioners a focused array of abilities, ranging from combat techniques or stealth, to mystical powers that align with their chosen path.

Talented (Levels 4, 8, 12, 16, and 19)

Choose a talent from the Martial Talents list or increase one ability modifier by +1.

Slow Fall (Level 4)

You can use your reaction when you fall, reducing any falling damage by five times your monk level.

Extra Attack (Level 5)

You can attack twice whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

Stunning Strike (Level 5)

Once per turn when you hit another creature with an unarmed or monk weapon attack, you can spend 1 ki point to attempt a stunning strike. In additional to the normal damage, the target must succeed on a CON saving throw or be stunned until the start of your next turn. Each time a stunned target takes damage, it can repeat the save. The amount of ki required increases by 1 for each creature size above Medium.

Ki-Empowered Strikes (Level 6)

Your unarmed strikes count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

Evasion (Level 6)

When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a DEX saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed, and only half damage if you fail.

Transcendent Movement (Level 9)

You gain the ability to move along vertical surfaces and across liquids on your turn without falling during the move. Your climbing speed is equal to your walking speed.

Heroic Boon (Level 10)

Choose one of the following heroic boons:

  • Purity of Body. Your mastery of the ki flowing through you makes you immune to disease and poison. Once per long rest, when you are reduced to 0 HP but not killed, you can instantly reset your current HP to your current number of ki points.
  • Purity of Mind. The clearness of your mind gives you advantage on Wisdom saves. As a bonus action, you can end one effect on yourself that is causing you to be charmed or frightened.

Sage Wisdom (Level 13)

As an action, you can expend 2 technique points to become proficient in one language, skill, tool, or weapon of your choice until you are incapacitated or use this feature again.

Resilient (Level 14)

Your mastery of energy grants you proficiency in all saves. When you fail a save, you can spend 1 ki point to reroll it, taking the second result.

Ageless Fortitude (Level 17)

Your spirit sustains you, body and mind. You gain the following benefits:

  • You can't be magically aged and suffer none of the frailty of old age. You can still die of old age, however.
  • You no longer need food or water.
  • Your ability scores and hit point maximum can't be lowered or reduced by any means short of a wish spell.

Spectral Savant (Level 18)

You can use your action to spend 4 ki points to become invisible for 1 minute. During that time, you also have resistance to all damage but force damage. In addition, you can spend 6 technique points to cast the True Seeing spell as an action without needing material components.

Epic Boon (Level 20)

Your constantly replenishing ki energy grants you a minimum of 2 ki points at the start of each of your turns in combat.

Your DEX and WIS modifiers increase by +1, up to a maximum of +6.

Monk Subclasses

Way of the Blade

The Way of the Blade teaches monks mastery of a wide array of weapons which they empower using their own energy, striking with unnatural speed and lethality.

Arsenalist (Level 3)

You gain proficiency in all weapons and you learn two additional ki techniques.

Nimble (Level 7)

Your initiative modifier is now equal to your DEX + WIS. You gain proficiency in two additional ki techniques.

Ascendant Weapon (Level 11)

As a bonus action, you can spend up to 3 ki points to enhance the power of your weapon strikes. The bonus equals the number of ki points you spent and applies to attack and damage rolls for 1 minute. This feature has no effect on a magic weapon that already has a bonus to attack or damage rolls.

Rending Resonance (Level 15)

When you successfully attack a creature on your turn, you can spend 4 ki points to leave a trace of energy behind that lasts for a number of days equal to your monk level.

For the duration, so long as you and the target are on the same plane, you can use an action to manipulate the trace of energy, ripping the creature apart. When you do, the target must make a CON save. On a failure, the target takes force damage equal to 5 × your monk level and is paralyzed until the end of your next turn. On a success, the target is stunned for 1 minute can repeat the save at the end of each of their turns or whenever they take damage. A target that fails the save by 5 or more instantly dies.

The ki point cost of this feature increases by +2 for each subsequent use and resets when you take a long rest. This feature can affect only one target at a time.

Way of the Ghost

The Way of the Ghost is a secretive order that focuses on stealth, deception, and deadly surprise attacks. They use secret techniques to communicate without words and often act as spies or assassins, blending into the shadows to achieve their goals.

Ghost Initiate (Level 3)

You learn a secret language called Shadow Tongue which is silent and based entirely on body language and facial expressions. Two ghosts can carry on a basic conversation without uttering a single word. You are proficient with the poisoner's kit. You also gain proficiency with blowguns and hand crossbows, which are monk weapons for you.

Ghost Stride (Level 3)

You gain expertise with the Stealth skill, doubling your PB on stealth checks. You can attempt to hide even when only lightly obscured, such as by foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, or mist. Additionally, you can spend 2 ki points to cast the Pass Without Trace or Silence spells.

Shroud Mastery (Level 7)

You gain expertise with the Slight of Hand skill, doubling your PB on relevant checks. You can also spend 2 ki points to cast the Fog Cloud or Misty Step spells.

Faceless (Level 11)

You gain expertise with the Deception skill, doubling your PB on relevant checks. You can spend 1 ki point to cast the Disguise Self spell and can change disguises at will for the duration of the spell.

Phantom Strike (Level 15)

When you attack while hidden or attack a creature that is surprised, you can spend 4 ki points to make a deadly strike. The target must make a CON saving throw. On a failed save, the damage of your attack is doubled and the creature is stunned until the end of your next turn. It can repeat the save at the end of each of its turn or whenever it takes damage. A target that fails the save by 5 or more instantly dies.

The ki point cost of this feature increases by +2 for each subsequent use and resets when you take a long rest.

Way of the Open Hand

Monks of the Open Hand hone their bodies to physical perfection. They learn techniques to push and trip their opponents, practice advanced meditation to protect them from harm, and develop secret techniques to transform their bodies into the ultimate weapon.

Focus Intent (Level 3)

When you or a creature within 5 feet of you makes an ability check or attack roll, you can use your reaction to roll your martial arts die. The target gains a bonus or a penalty (your choice) to its roll equal to the number you rolled. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your WIS modifier. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Open Hand Technique (Level 3)

You can manipulate your enemy's energy with your own. When you hit a creature with one of the attacks granted by your Flurry of Blows technique, you can impose one of the following effects on that target:

  • It can't take reactions until the end of your next turn.
  • It must succeed on a DEX save or be knocked prone.
  • It must succeed on a STR save or be pushed up to 15 feet away from you.

Wholeness of Body (Level 7)

When you roll initiative and have at least 1 hit point, you regain a number of HP equal to your CON + monk level.

Tranquil Soul (Level 11)

You have learned the way of war, so you may better walk the world in peace. As an action, you can spend 3 technique points to cast the sanctuary spell on yourself. The spell lasts until the end of your next short or long rest (or it can end early as normal).

Quivering Palm (Level 15)

As an action, you can make a single unarmed strike against a creature. On a hit, you can spend 4 technique points to fill that creature with potentially lethal vibrations that last for a number of days equal to your monk level.

For the duration, so long as you and the target are on the same plane, you can use an action to end the vibrations, ripping the creature apart. When you do, the target must make a CON save. On a failure, the target takes force damage equal to 5 × your monk level and is paralyzed until the end of your next turn. On a success, the target is stunned for 1 minute, and at the end of each of their turns, and each time they take damage, the target can repeat the save, ending the effect on a success. A target that fails the save by 5 or more instantly dies.

Each time you use this feature after the first, the technique cost to use it increases by 2. When you finish a long rest, the cost resets. This feature can affect only one target at a time.

Way of the Sentinel

The Way of the Sentinel is a protective path, focusing on healing, defending allies, and enhancing their abilities. Sentinels excel at preventing harm, providing sanctuary, and empowering those around them, embodying the ideal guardian who stands vigilant and ready to protect at all costs.

Martial Medic (Level 3)

You gain proficiency with light and medium armor, and with the Medicine skill. You can use your bonus action to spend 1 ki point and heal a creature you touch by a number of HP equal to your martial arts die + WIS.

Sentinel's Refuge (Level 7)

You can use your bonus action to spend 1 ki point and cast the Sanctuary spell, warding yourself or an ally within 30 feet from attack. Once per round when an enemy within 30 feet of you attacks one of your allies, you can immediately make a ranged attack against them or move to a space adjacent to the attacker and make one melee attack.

Serene Empathy (Level 11)

You can spend 2 ki points to cast the Warding Bond, Calm Emotions, or Detect Thoughts spells. You can use your bonus action to remove the Charmed or Frightened condition from your allies.

Empowering Aura (Level 15)

As long as you are conscious, you and any allies within 30 feet benefit from a +1 AC bonus as well as a +1d4 bonus to attacks and saving throws.
